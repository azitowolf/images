EF Tech Recruitment Site

{Interactive website and UX}

The Project

Education First is an education company, but we also want to be a frontrunner when it comes to the educational technology industry. This all starts with a strong tech recruitment process, so my team was asked to put together a microsite for us to use to funnel EF labs applicants the careers website. We needed to show off our process and our work culture, and do it all in the span of 3 weeks.

The tagline of the site was "small teams, big problems", and we created several custom images to illustrate this theme.

My Role

My Role - for this project I was the technical lead, in charge of representing the technological side of the business to a team full of designers and business analysts. My responsibility was to represent the technological capabilities of our team during the design phase of the product, and then during implementation to pick the optimal technology and translate all that creative thought into a tangible product for our users.

Challenges

Representing EF’s culture - It’s a really tough thing to boil down an entire departments culture into the space of a single web page. We thought about many ways to do this, including using pictures of us actually building the website in the website - so meta! What we ended up deciding was that we wanted to capture two things about our process in the tech department - the first is that we work in small multidisciplinary teams, and the second that we are solving large complicated problems. Thanks to our talented copywriter, we settled on the catchphrase “small teams, big problems”.

Finding the right technology - Because this microsite was going to be viewed by thousands of software engineers from around the world, we needed it to be built elegantly and with the right technology. Basically, we needed to show off our coding chops. I decided to build the site in React.js, and I built a plugin that I posted on Github afterword, so that any interested developers could follow a trail of breadcrumbs to understand my thought process in building the site.

Building for marketers - We needed to make the site content-editable so that designers, copywriters and marketers could change the content later. Myself and another developer built our own data storage system to allow the site to be editable in the future without any technical knowledge. The marketers were really thankful!

The Solution

We built a totally independent microsite, built with advanced technology and designed to showcase our creative thinking. The site linked back to the EF careers page at different points to drive interested techies towards our jobs.
